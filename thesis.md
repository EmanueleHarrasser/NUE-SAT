"While recent literature (Selsam, Kurin) has successfully demonstrated that Graph Neural Networks can reduce the search space of DPLL/CDCL solvers, they suffer from fatal wall-clock inference bottlenecks. Drawing inspiration from modern chess engines, this thesis introduces an network (Neural Network Updated Evaluation) architecture to Automated Theorem Proving. By utilizing an incremental feature accumulator and Expert Iteration, we achieve search space reduction while maintaining the CPU-level microsecond inference required for practical SAT solving."
